    Spain IVF legal restrictions & laws

    Every form of fertility treatment in Spain is regulated by national laws, including 14/2006 (ART Law). Spain is a Christian country, but they have a very open approach to all kinds of infertility treatment.

    For example, unmarried couples, singles, or anyone in the LGBT community can receive treatment.

    They have stricter laws regarding Egg donors. These donors have guaranteed anonymity, new parents will know the age and blood type of the donor, and the offspring will have non-identifying information to help find genetic siblings. Egg Donors have also limited 6 live births.

    A maximum of three embryos may be transferred and gender selection is only allowed in the case of inherited gender-related diseases.


    General Information

    The Spain, officially the Kingdom of Spain, is a country located on the Iberian Peninsula. It borders Portugal to the west, Andorra and France to the northeast, and Gibraltar to the south; the Spanish North African states of Ceuta and Melilla have a land border with Morocco

    The Kingdom of Spain also includes the Canary Islands in the Atlantic Ocean and the Balearic Islands in the Mediterranean. Part of Spain is the Catalan city of Llívia, which is completely surrounded by France.


    History

    The oldest fragments of human bones discovered in Europe were found in the Atapuerca Cave near Burgos in Spain. The find, which is about 800,000 years old, testifies to the very ancient settlement of the Iberian Peninsula.

    Another important historical event occurred in 1492, when the sailor Christopher Columbus discovered the New World, thus beginning the era of the Spanish Empire. For the next few centuries, Spain was the largest power in Europe and around the world.

    Spain is a constitutional monarchy, a member of the European Union, NATO and other organizations. According to the 1978 Democratic Constitution, Spanish (Castilian) is the official language throughout the country; other languages are recognized as official by the individual Autonomous Communities.

    Bullfighting (corrida) is associated with the Spanish tradition. However, they do not take place everywhere. They are even banned in Catalonia.
