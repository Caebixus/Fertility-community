3 Reasons To Choose IVF in Prague


  1. Short Waiting Time for IVF with Egg Donation

    Due to the law in the Czech Republic, all donors are required to donate anonymously. This law can help encourage fertile young people to donate their eggs, leading to shorter waiting times for IVF in Prague with egg donation.

  2. Affordable Treatments and Travel Costs

    Due to the lower cost of living in Prague compared to other major European cities, such as London or Paris, the costs of IVF in Prague are more affordable. However, this price is not reflective of the quality. The standard of care is high as the clinics pride themselves on their incredible bedside manner and medical expertise.

    You also don’t need to worry about substantial travel costs to Prague when considering IVF abroad. As a popular tourist destination, flights and accommodation in Prague are generally within most couples budgets. Moreover, travel to Prague is incredibly convenient as most flights from Europe will go direct and won’t be longer than a few hours at most.

  3. Beautiful and Laidback City

    Of course, when your main goal is finding the right clinic for you, the location matters less. However, it never hurts to be in a beautiful place where you feel at home and well-rested before your consultations and treatments. Therefore, we recommend a 7-10 day stay in Prague to have enough time to settle in, make all your appointments and do a spot of sightseeing in the meantime.

    Not only is this an excellent time to spend bonding with your partner, but you can also benefit from the low-stress environment, which may improve your treatment process. In addition, your physical and mental health state can impact your success rates, so this is a wonderful bonus to booking IVF in Prague.

Success Rates of IVF in Prague


As mentioned in our previous post on IVF in the Czech Republic, IVF success rates at various clinics are not on a public database. This lack of public data can make it more challenging to use success rates as a comparison factor when considering which country and clinic to undergo your fertility treatments.


However, the European Social of Human Reproduction and Embryology (ESHRE) has reported that success rates in the Czech Republic are good and generally better than countries that are more well-known for their health care, such as the UK.





IVF Limitations in Prague


As per the legal requirements for a couple to undergo IVF treatment within the Czech Republic, you must be in a heterosexual relationship and be living together. Therefore, this law prohibits homosexual couples from receiving fertility treatments in the country.


As a mature woman seeking fertility treatment in Prague, you must be under 48 years and 394 days old to receive an egg donation for your IVF treatments.


In Prague, all egg and sperm donors remain entirely anonymous, so you may wish to reconsider IVF in Prague if it is essential to you or your partner to know the biological profile of the donor.
